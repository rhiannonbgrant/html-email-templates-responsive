Foods that you can include to get more Calcium, Vitamin E and Vitamin K

Kale

They call it the queen of greens and I can't think of a vegetable more deserving of this noble title. Kale is a real winner when it comes to Vitamin K, just measley 12g's is enough to get your RDI for Vitamin K, thats quite incredible. Its also high in Calcium, potassium, fiber and so many more great nutrients. Read more about it here. Eat fresh and cooked, mix it up as the cooking process destroys some of its nutrient and make others more bioavailable. Eat it, drink it, wear it on your t-shirt, it's all good. Green smoothies and kale salads can be a great raw to get your raw kale in, and as for cooked, like most vegetables the skies the limit on what you can do with it, its great in asian dishes, soups, pastas and curries.

Sunflower Seeds

Sunflower seeds are the most concentrated source of Vitamin E in the common foods that we eat. The best way to eat them is raw as the cooking process decrease vitamins E content by 17%, but if like them roasted and won't eat them raw than roasted still way better than not at all. 60g of sunflower is enough to get your full days worth of Vitamin E, and amount will also contribute very significant quantities of other essential and sometimes difficult to get your full RDI of nutrients, such as Manganese (58.8%), Magnesium (48.8%), Selenium (45.4%), Folate (34.1%), Copper (54%) B1 (Thamine 59.2%), B6 (pyridoxine 40.4%), Zinc (20%).

Of course, 60gs of sunflower seeds each day is a lot, but you don't have to eat 60g's of sunflower everyday, these number are just to illustrate how high in a variety of essential nutrients sunflower seeds really are. Just try to include a bit each day in order to boost your intake of all these great nutrients. Other foods to include high in Vitamin E are Almonds, hazelnuts, pine nuts, brazil nuts, peanuts, avocados, spinach, kale and other dark leafy greens. Include a variety of these foods in you're diet each day and you will be set for Vitamin E. There are so many ways to include sunflower seeds in your diet, they are great as a snack, in salads, in granola, in desserts and as a garnish on just about anything.

Tofu

Tofu is a healthy food and a rich source of calcium. Specially tofu set in Calcium salt, which is most regular tofu. To achieve you full RDI of calcium from tofu you'd need to eat 270g of tofu, which is more tofu than most people can eat in a day unless you love tofu. 125g of tofu is a much more sensible portion size, and will get you to 47%. In order to get you full RDI, include other calcium rich foods in you diet, like all the dark leafy greens, dairy products, black eyed peas parsley and butternut squash.

Eating 125g of tofu will also provide significant quantities of protein (33%), copper (17%), iron (19%), phosphorus (19%), selenium (30.9%), choline (10%), B5 pantothenic Acid (14%), manganese (55%), zinc (12%) and potassium (6%). On top of all that, it has a great distribution of all the different amino acids. Tofu is delicious and has been used in Asian cooking for over a thousand years. Of course, tofu is in many ways a blank canvas and these days there are thousands of recipes asian and not that use tofu as a principle ingredient.
