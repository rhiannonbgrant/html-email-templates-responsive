The Macaw is probably the most iconic rainforest bird that most of us know of. They are captivatingly beautiful as well as being surprisingly clever. Unfortunately this means that they have that there is a thriving illegal trapping market which exist that is driving many Macaw species towards extinction in the wild. There are many species of these fascinating birds living throughout central and south America.

Their colourful plumage is not only attractive to as an enticement to mate but also blend well into a forest colourful fruit and flowers. They can live to a grand old age of 80 years, which means that if you do happen to have one of these majestic birds as a pet unless you are young, you're bird is likely to outlive you.

Chameleons

Chameleons are so different to other reptiles and lizard, they seems as if they are from another planet. Everything about them is awesome; they have have feet that operate like salad tongs, independently operating eyes that give tham 360 degrees vision and a long elastic tongue that that is one of the fastest tongues in the animal kingdom and can extend to twice its body-length. Of course, this isn't even mentioning what make their coolest feature; their ability to change colour. Theories as how chameleons achieve the they spectacular display and for what reason boiled down to pure speculation until recently.

In 2015, Researchers discovered that the superficial layer under the skin of chameleons has pigments under it and under that lies a lattice of guanine nanocrystals. Chameleons change colour by actually tuning the spacing between these guanine crystals. Changing the spaces between these crystal changes the wavelength of light that's reflected off of those crystals. Of course, other big question around chameleon and this incredible capacity to change colour and such intricate patterns is why?
